# 12. Stadium
Your stadium's capacity is another decisive factor in Planetarium Manager. This is because the higher your stadium's capacity is, the higher the income from the attendance will be – providing, of course, that your team is able to attract the necessary number of supporters to fill the stadium!

The downside of having a big stadium is that you'll have to pay the maintenance costs. Be careful with unnecessary seats (also known as terraces). As we said, the stadium might not always sell out every match. Empty seats are a loss of income. But if you have your stadium full on every match, it also means that you are most likely losing money, because you are not taking full advantage of the fact that with a bigger stadium you would probably have a larger attendance.

The stadium is divided into 8 stands, and you can build as many seats as you want. The specific Stand that you decide to upgrade is also up to you. This is only for visual purposes because, for example, the North Stand will not attract more fans to your stadium than the East Stand. The construction of new seats isn't immediate, and it can last several game months, depending of course on the dimension of the new capacity. Remember that a Planetarium Manager month is equal to a real-life week (more information can be found in Section 33).

You also have the possibility to reduce the stadium's capacity. For that, just change the option “Type of Construction” to “Demolish”. As in the construction type, you will have to pay a fee for each demolished seat. The seat destruction is immediate.If you decide to demolish 15,000 seats they will be gone as soon as you confirm the demolition.

The price for each seat built is 20€. The cost for a demolition is 5€ per seat.

You cannot set the ticket price for your stadium. The price is constant, and we will not be changing the ticket prices any time soon (unless it is absolutely needed).

Pitch Types
All clubs will start with a grassy pitch by default.
Clubs in the top 2 divisions will be obligated to have a grassy pitch and this change will be automatic once a team is promoted to these divisions.
Clubs from the bottom divisions will have the option to downgrade their pitch to "dirt".
Upgrading or downgrading your pitch will take about 2 game-months (2 real-life weeks) and will cost 10000€.

See Section 14 for more information on this feature.

Stadium Cover
Clubs will have the option to build a stadium cover. This will cost about 400000€ to build, and about 10000€ per game month/1 real-life week to maintain. They will take 2 game months to be ready, and no club will start with a cover. This must be purchased.

A stadium cover can be built regardless of the division the club is playing in, and regardless of the type of pitch/size of the stadium.

For more information on this feature please see Section 14.

You can find all stadium related options under Team >> Structures.
All stadium updates are completed on Fridays during the economical updates for your country.

Rival Clubs
Rivalry, in PM, is decided by a series of different factors:
- Matches between clubs, alone, increase their rivalry.
- If you play MOTS against another club, it will greatly affect your rivalry with that club. The club’s rivalry with you is unaffected, though.

Rivalry will affect directly the number of supporters you attract to a match. If, for instance, club A is rivals with club B, club A will attract a larger crowd to a match between those clubs. If both clubs are rivals of each other, the attendance bonus will be on both parts, clubs A and B will attract larger crowds to the match.
