# 17. Teams
Teams are entities controlled by users (human managers) or by the computer (Bots). Bot teams do not have a very refined AI (Artificial Intelligence). The most they'll do is select the starting eleven - nothing more.

Each team is created with 22 players, and cannot have more than 50 players or less than 11.

Team Rankings
Each team has its own ranking value (often called reputation). This ranking is limited (from 0 to 100) and your division also limits your team's reputation. For example, the max reputation a team playing in the first division can have will always be bigger than the max reputation a team playing in the second division can have.
Team ranking has influence on several key aspects of PManager such as:
- Players contracts: players will ask for higher wages to teams with higher reputation.
- Cup qualification: teams with a lower reputation may not qualify for the cup (check Section 22 for more information),
- Wage roof: a better reputation will mean a higher wage roof.
- International team competitions: the lowest ranked team will always play at home in WCC.

You might be wondering how this ranking/reputation system changes. The answer is quite simple: only based on results in league and cup matches. You can find information on how many points your team will win or lose (depending on the result) by looking at the “Next Match” option. These points are the same for the manager ranking as well.

Supporters
Each team has its own number of supporters. In PManager this information is given in a text scale on your team's details page.
The number of supporters a team has will directly influence match attendance. The only way to increase your number of supporters is by winning matches – as many “in a row” as you can. You should also know that each division has its average number of supporters, and it will be hard to have a number of supporters higher than the average of your division. In order to achieve that, you should make good runs with positive results. Here's the average number of supporters for each division:
- First: 30,000
- Second: 25,000
- Third: 20,000
- Fourth: 15,000
- Fifth (and below): 10,000

I received a new team. Why isn't my team in the first division?
Our policy is to put newbies in lower divisions. It's better for their learning process and it would also be unfair to those managers who have already played for a long time. Reaching the first division should be earned, not “given” to any team. The system depends on the number of divisions a country has. Let's cover all of the cases:
- One division only: Newbie managers will be placed in this division
- Two divisions: Newbie managers can be placed in these two divisions
- Three divisions (or more): Newbie managers will be placed in the last two divisions only.
