# 25. International Team Competitions
International competitions will take place during the same time as tournament games (Fridays at 13:00 server time), and teams playing in these competitions will be automatically dropped from their tournaments (they will lose every missing tournament game by walk-over). Injuries and attendance income will also be following tournament rules. The only difference is that all games will give some experience points to the players; experience points in the final knock-out phases of the CWL and WCL will be doubled, and will also be doubled in the last four rounds of the WCC. It will be possible to play Match of the Season games in international competitions, but these will be deducted from the club's available MOTS: meaning the “two per season” maximum still applies. The final match will always be played on a neutral, grassy-pitched stadium.

An international team's ranking exists, similar to the national teams ranking, called... international club ranking. The position of a country on this ranking will define the number of teams from that country that will be qualified to the new international competitions. The WCL and CWL group phases start at the 3rd week of the season (1st week with league matches) and the WCC on the 4th week.

So, the new competitions are:

World Champions League (WCL)
All of the top league champions from all PM countries will be qualified to play in this competition.
The competition will have a group stage, and a final knock-out stage. This will be modeled almost in the same fashion as the World Cup qualifying and final stages. In the group stage all 60 teams will play; the only difference to the World Cup qualifiers is that there will only be 1 round of games (the teams will play against each other only once). All of the group winners, plus the 7 best 2nd placed teams (a total of 16 teams) will qualify to the knock-out stage, exactly like in the World Cup.
The winner prizes for the finalists will be: 600000€ to the World Champion; 400000€ to the second place team.

Cup Winners League (CWL)
Same rules as WCL, except the teams taking place in this one will be the cup winners from all countries. In case the cup winner also won the top league, they will take part in the WCL instead, and the other cup finalist will be qualified to the CWL.
The winner prizes for the finalists will be: 200000€ to the CWL winner; 100000€ to the second-placed team. The reason for the low prizes here is mainly that the national cups already have a higher prize compared to the top league champion prize.

World Club Cup (WCC)
This will be a knock-out competition only, national-cup-style, with no group stages. 256 teams will be qualified to play in this cup, and they will be chosen as follows:
- 9 teams from the top 8 countries according to the new club ranking (2nd to 6th placed from the top division plus the 4 B division winners; in case any of these is qualified to the CWL cup the 7th placed from the top division is qualified instead of it).
- 5 teams from the next 12 countries on the ranking (2nd to 6th placed from the top division, 7th placed if any of the others takes part on the CWL).
- 4 teams from the next 16 countries (2nd to 5th from top div., 6th if any of the others are unavailable);
- 3 teams from the next 12 countries (2nd to 4th from top div., 5th if any of the others is unavailable);
- 2 teams from the bottom 12 countries (2nd and 3rd from top div., 4th if any of the others is unavailable).
The lowest ranked team will always play at home.
The winner prizes for the finalists will be: 600000€ to the WCC winner; 400000€ to the second-placed team.
