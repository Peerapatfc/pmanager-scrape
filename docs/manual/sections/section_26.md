# 26. National Federations & National Teams
National Federation


Just like in real-life, there are National Federation (and Presidents) in PManager. Their task is to appoint CTs for National Teams, change the national teams' kits and initiate referendums for changing the federal rules. There will be a national federation for each country. Each federation has a president, elected by the national users.
Here is some info about this feature:

The election of the president works like this:
- You can vote only concerning the federation of your country;
- You can vote on any national user that has come forward as a candidate;
- You cannot vote for yourself.
- In case of a draw in an election, the user that applied to the position first will be settled as winner

(where applicable, these last items are valid both for National Federation and PIF elections)

Users, after the election starts, will need to officially state they’re candidates so other users can vote for them. There’s a period of 3 days for candidates to present themselves before the real election starts. The candidate with more votes after the election period of 7 days is the new president.

The president of the National Federation will be the user responsible for nominating the National Team managers of the country (both the main NT & U21 managers). The president can name whoever he wants, of any nationality, except he can not nominate himself; a president can’t ever be national manager at the same time. The user nominated for the manager job will still need to accept the proposal, of course. The president will also be responsible for the firing of the National Managers, and will be able to do it anytime he wishes.

Other presidential tasks include:
- All presidents will decide amongst them who will host the next World Cups (using an internal voting system);
- Presidents will be able to choose the stadium used for NT games: the national stadium;
- Presidents have the ability to change the name of the first division of the country by creating a national referendum in which all users from the country can vote;
- Presidents can change the name of the national federation, also subject to national referendum.
- Presidents can set the national team kits.
- Presidents can set the national URL, the federation logo and a lot of other minor options.
- Presidents can send national announcements to the community.
- Presidents can nominate vice-presidents: these have all the options the president has except creating referendums, firing/nominating National Managers and voting for the World Cup site. They can’t be National Managers either.

National referendums are open for 7 days and can only be made once a month (for each type of referendum).

A president is elected "for life"; there’s only two ways to get him off the presidential position. One of the ways is simple: he quits the job himself. The other is through a "revolution". A revolution works like this: Every election, the president is elected with a certain number of votes. After he’s elected, people can choose either to support the current president or to support a revolution (in the federation page). If the number of users supporting a revolution is greater than 75% of the votes the president got on the last election, a revolution takes place and a new election is started. If the number of votes he got elected with exceeds the number of users in the country, the number of users will be applied instead (so, if 75% of the total number of users are against the president, a revolution will occur).
During an election the president cannot use any of his powers. If the election was started because he resigned, nobody will be able to vote for him (he won’t be able to set himself up as a candidate). In case of a revolution, he can take part as a candidate if he wishes to do so.

This will obviously deeply change the current NT managers system, but will hopefully work more sensibly and give the users more power over who is in charge of their national teams.
This will be also be an important base for a lot of planned features. New referendums will be added to the federation’s list of actions as these new features are developed and laucnhed. In the future, our plan is to give the federation alot of powers concerning specific country-based rules.
National Teams


Just like in real-life, there are National Teams (NTs) in PManager. In fact, there are two NTs per country: the main NT and the U21 NT. The cool thing about National Teams, besides representing your own community, is that they are managed by human managers. They are appointed by Presidents of National Federations.

Every two seasons, the World Cup (WC) occurs. As the WC and the U21 WC are not played during the same season, this means that, at the end of every season there's a World Cup: either the WC or the U21 WC.

How does a World Cup work?
The first phase after the elections are over is the “friendly phase”. For 8 straight weeks, managers will have the possibility to build their squad in friendly matches. It's usually during these matches that managers test the abilities of their NTs so they can prepare for the Qualifying Stage (QS).

The Qualifying Stage happens in 10 matches with groups of 6 teams (home and away matches). The top NT will qualify for the World Cup and then, depending on the number of groups, the best 6 second placed NTs will qualify as well. These groups are seeded based on the National Team ranking (explained further down this section). The seed method is quite easy to understand: NTs are divided into pots of 6 teams depending on their ranking (first 6 in pot 1, next 6 in pot 2, etc); then, the draw will pick one team for each pot in order to make all groups. This will ensure that the top NTs won't have to face each other in the Qualifying stage in order to make things more interesting and balanced.

After the QS is completed, the draw to the WC is made. 16 teams will be qualified for the knock-out stage. The first round of draw will try to avoid clashes between the first placed on all groups. After the first round of the draw is played, all of the next 3 rounds are seeded randomly. As you're already aware at this time, there are 4 elimination rounds in the WC. The winner of the final match can then be considered the best NT of the game!

When are World Cups played?
At the beginning of each season, right after the new season's transition.

What's the difference between main NTs and U21 NTs?
The difference is obvious, for all of you who are familiar with International Football. U21 teams can only have players with 21 years old or less. Main NTs can have players from all ages.

Then why do I see U21 teams with players over 21 years old?
That's a special case, and only happens during the U21 World Cup – knock-out stage. As players age at the season transition, it would be extremely unfair to “lock” players that were 21 years old in the previous season who started the U21 campaign as 20 year olds. So, U21 National Team managers will be able to have these players in their squad during the WC only. However, and this is very important, they have to make sure that they are in the team before the new season transition occurs. They won't be able to call them up again once they are 22. So they must make sure that they are in their NT squads for the final group stage match in order to be available for the knock-out stage.

So, the time needed to play all Friendly Matches, Qualifying and World Cup is 2 regular seasons?
Yes, that's it.

What are the benefits of having players in the National Teams?
Your players will get extra experience, and (usually) international players are sold for higher prices on the transfer market. Those are the only in-game advantages for International Players. Getting players on an NT is also very prestigious for the owners of those players.

Do players get injured in National Teams matches?
Yes, they do.

Can NT managers use MOTS in their matches?
Yes, they can. A National Team has the possibility to use 2 MOTS in the friendly + qualifying stage. MOTS can't be used in the final stage of the World Cup. They can use PIC whenever they want; however, the experience gained while using PIC is less than if you played normal or MOTS.

What are the most important things NT managers should know?
Besides the basic (tactics, ME, etc) they should know that:
- National teams have a temporary squad that will later be released in order to form a definitive squad.
- The definitive squad cannot be released more than 6 days before the match.
- Only 22 players (max) can be selected for a definitive squad. The min. is 16 players.
- After the definitive squad is released, managers have access to all tactical options.
- Injured & suspended players can be called up; however, they obviously cannot play.
- Players cannot be in the main and U21 squad at the same time. If you don't see the option to call up a player then it is probably because he's already on the other squad. There should be coordination between the main and U21 manager regarding the players that can be “shared” between squads.
- National Team matches are played on Fridays at 20:00 (server time). World Cup matches are played as follows:
- Eight-Finals are played on the first Wednesday of the next season;
- Quarter-Finals are played on the first Friday;
- Semi-Finals are played on the second Wednesday;
- The Final is played on the second Friday. All matches are 20:00 server time.
- The definitive squad announced for the World Cup (5 days or less before the first match) cannot be changed until the end of the World Cup.
- International Team matches can only be played on grassy pitches.
- Free players can be called up to National temporary squads, but they won't be able to take part in the definitive squad once you release it.

How does the ranking for the NTs work?
Each international match will generate ranking points for both U21 and NTs. The ranking will work as follows:
>> Points for result:
- Win (no penalty shootout) 3
- Draw 1
- Loss (no penalty shootout) 0
- Win (penalty shootout) 2
- Loss (penalty shootout) 1

>> The value will then be multiplied as follows:
- Game type: Friendly match: x1 | WC Qualifier: x2 | WC Stage: x4
- Opponent’s position ranking with the following formula, where:
¤¤¤¤ X = Number of Countries in PM | Y = Opponent’s Ranking Position
¤¤¤¤ Multiplier = (X - (Y - 1)) / (X / 2) (This multiplier cannot be lower than 0.5)

So, the amount of ranking points if you play and win an NT Friendly against the first ranked team:
>> 3 (winning points) x 1 (game type mult.) x 2 (ranking mult.) = 6 points
In a World Cup final stage >> 3 x 4 (game type mult.) x 2 = 24 points

This version is actually very similar to the formula used by FIFA. We have removed some parts in order to add simplicity.

The ranking will work on an NT season basis (an NT season is made up of 2 regular seasons). This ranking will be reset once the World Cup finishes (remember that NT's seasons are only increased once a WC is completed). However, the points are saved until 2 seasons in the past, and will be used to define the final ranking position.

An example:
- The main English NT wins 40 points in season 1, 30 points in season 2 and 50 in season 3. Their ranking points are the sum of these 3 seasons (120). Then, in season 4 they get 80 points. Season 1 points are dropped, and the ranking points are updated to 30+50+80=160.
