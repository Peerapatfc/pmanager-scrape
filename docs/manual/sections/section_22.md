# 22. National Cup
Each country will have its own national cup competition. Of course, the number of rounds in the cup will depend on the number of teams in that country. We'll present you with a relation between the number of teams in a country, and the number of rounds/teams the national cup will have:
10 Teams - 3 Rounds - 8 Teams in the National Cup
50 Teams - 5 Rounds - 32 Teams in the National Cup
210 Teams - 7 Rounds - 128 Teams in the National Cup
850 Teams - 9 Rounds - 512 Teams in the National Cup
3,410 Teams - 11 Rounds – 2,048 Teams in the National Cup
13,650 Teams - 13 Rounds – 8,192 Teams in the National Cup

As you can see in almost all cases, some teams won't be able to participate in the cup. To get the necessary number of teams of a country where all teams are not able to participate in the cup we use a ranking system (explained in Section 17).

Another important thing to be mentioned is concerning cup gate receipts. 60% of the gate receipts of cup matches will be given to the home team, and 40% to the away team. If we're talking about the final, the receipts will be shared (50%/ 50%).

Cup matches are special matches. If the game is not settled after 90' it will go into extra-time. If the team's are still tied after 120' then there will be a penalty shoot-out. The winning team will move on to the next round.

As told before, cup matches are played mid-week on Mondays or Tuesdays (depending on your country's match schedule). A team cannot play a friendly and a cup match on the same week – as these are played on the same day. During off-season, teams might be required to play cup-ties during the weekends. This will only happen in countries with 5 divisions or more.

Players train after cup matches. However, only those who have played at least 30 minutes in the game will train. For more information about training and cup matches please read Section 8.

Section 7 will talk about player experience gained during Cup matches.
Read about Cup Matches and suspensions in Section 14.

The cup winner will be given a monetary prize of € 800,000. The runner-up will earn € 400,000.
